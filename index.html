Program Design Logic

The Whys Behind Our Design Choices

Every element of our program structure is intentional. Here's the theory behind our design choices.

Why Weekly for 6 Months

Ritual builds community.

You can't build a real network in a one-off intensive. Weekly sessions create rhythm, familiarity, and repeated contact that turns strangers into trusted peers. By session 20, they're a communityânot just a cohort.

Transformation requires sustained engagement.

Mindset shifts don't happen in a weekend. The 6-month arc gives leaders time to encounter new frameworks, try them in their real work, reflect on what happened, and try again. This is the difference between inspiration (which fades) and transformation (which sticks).

Developmental arc over time.

The Hope to ActionÂ® curriculum follows a deliberate sequence through the 4 P's: early sessions focus on Power (Leading Self â grounding in values, identity, courage), middle sessions on Polarities and Pragmatism (Leading Together â engaging diverse perspectives, building collective power), and later sessions on Peacebuilding (Leading Change â turning hope into tangible, sustainable progress).

Why Saturdays at 10am Singapore Time

Inclusion of working adults and parents.

Our leaders are busyârunning organizations, serving in government, leading movements. Saturday mornings allow participation without sacrificing careers or family responsibilities. A weekday program would exclude many of the leaders we most want to reach.

Spanning 19 time zones.

Our cohorts include leaders from Hawai'i (UTC-10) to Kiribati (UTC+14)âa 19-timezone spread. Sessions at 10am Singapore time represent a carefully calculated compromise, with program staff and facilitators joining from Washington DC, Chicago, and Los Angeles.

Why Community Groups

The small group as the unit of belonging.

Inspired by Peter Block's work on community, we recognize that deep trust and vulnerability require intimate settings. Full cohort sessions of 35 can inspire and teachâbut belonging happens in circles of 5-6. The Community Group is each leader's first line of belonging to the broader cohort and Obama Leaders community.

Four integrated objectives:
Ground Create a Sense of Belonging

Encourage leaders to show up fully and build trust by connecting beyond professional roles and identities. This small group is each leader's first line of belonging to the broader cohort and Obama Leaders community.

Collaborate Deepen Relationships Across Difference

Practice pluralism and navigate productive tension with diverse peers. Bear witness to one another's growthâbuilding bonds and trust that last for the long arc of change.

Transform Integrate Learning in Context

Support leaders in making sense of their learning journey and applying program insights within the realities of their own communities and systems.

Alumni-facilitated, monthly rhythm.

Community Groups meet roughly monthly, facilitated by program alumni who model the leadership we cultivate. These sessions provide space for the kind of honest conversation that transforms how leaders see themselves and each other.

5-6 Leaders per Group â By Graduation, Each Feels "Seen" by These Peers

Why 1:1 Coaching

Individualized reflection space.

Group sessions can't provide the personal attention each leader needs. Coaching offers dedicated space to process learnings, connect frameworks to personal context, and work through the specific challenges each leader faces.

Depth requires dedicated coaches.

Foundation program staff cannot provide this level of individualized support to 35 leaders simultaneously. Our coaching teamâwith each coach supporting a maximum of 5 leadersâensures every leader receives sustained, personal attention throughout the program.

Three integrated coaching objectives:

Ground Deepen Self-Awareness & Values-Based Leadership

Support leaders in clarifying their personal leadership values, purpose, and goalsâand help them progress toward those goals with greater integrity.

Collaborate Strengthen Adaptive & Relational Capacity

Support leaders in seeing and pursuing new opportunities to navigate complexity, hold difference, and build trust across diverse communities.

Transform Build Resilience for the Long Arc

Support leaders to integrate reflective and regenerative practices to sustain impactâand themselvesâover time.

Practice ground for readiness.

Between sessions, leaders are already leading. Coaching helps them apply frameworks in their current contextsâbuilding the muscle memory they'll draw on when bigger moments arrive.

5:1 Maximum Leader-to-Coach Ratio

Why In-Person at the End

Virtual builds relationship; in-person makes it real.

After 5 months of weekly connection, leaders arrive already knowing each other's stories, struggles, and contexts. The convening transforms digital relationships into embodied onesâthe kind of connection that sustains across years and distance.

Culmination, not introduction.

Many programs start with an intensive then struggle to maintain momentum. We invert this: the in-person week is earned, anticipated, and serves as a capstone that cements the community and launches leaders into their next chapter.

Respecting leaders' constraints.

Asking leaders to take a full week away from work and family is significant. By placing it at the endâonce trust is built and value is demonstratedâwe honor that sacrifice and ensure leaders arrive ready to be fully present.

Why 35 Leaders Across Sectors & Countries

Diversity is the curriculum.

Leaders learn pluralism by practicing it. A cohort spanning 38 countries, multiple sectors, and diverse issue areas means every session is an exercise in engaging across differenceânot as theory, but as lived experience.

The cohort as practice ground.

We teach leaders to bridge across difference, build trust with those unlike them, and find common ground amid divergent perspectives. The cohort itself becomes the space to practice these skillsâmaking the learning embodied, not abstract.

Network effects multiply impact.

When a Filipino education leader knows Thai climate activists, Indonesian civic technologists, and Australian community organizers, the potential for collaboration expands exponentially. The cohort becomes infrastructure for future action.
